1. INTRODUCTION
Welcome to TechSphere Inc. This document serves as the central repository for all corporate policies, technical guidelines, and onboarding information.

2. LEAVE POLICY & ATTENDANCE
TechSphere values work-life balance. The following leave policy applies to all full-time employees:
- Earned Leaves (EL): Employees are allowed 15 earned leaves per year.
- Casual Leaves (CL): Employees are allowed 8 casual leaves per year for personal matters.
- Sick Leaves (SL): Employees are entitled to 10 sick leaves annually. Medical certificates are required for sick leaves exceeding 3 consecutive days.
To apply for a leave, employees must submit a request via the HR portal (WorkSphere) at least 48 hours in advance for CL and EL.

3. REMOTE WORK & INTERNSHIP RULES
TechSphere operates on a hybrid model for full-time employees (3 days in-office, 2 days remote).
Regarding our internship program:
- Interns are expected to be present in the office 5 days a week for the first month for training and onboarding.
- After the first month, interns can work remotely for up to 2 days a week, subject to their manager's approval.
- Interns must use company-provided laptops for all development work; personal devices are strictly prohibited due to security compliance.

4. TECHNICAL DOCUMENTATION & IT PROTOCOLS
This section outlines critical IT processes for the engineering team.

4.1 Database Backup Process
To ensure data integrity, the database backup process must be strictly followed:
1. Automated daily snapshots are taken at 02:00 AM UTC via AWS RDS.
2. For manual backups before major deployments, engineers must use the `pg_dump` utility and store the encrypted output in the designated secure S3 bucket (`techsphere-db-backups`).
3. The retention period for all database backups is 90 days.
4. The DevOps lead must perform a quarterly backup restoration drill to verify data integrity.

4.2 Server Deployment
To deploy the backend server to production, merge your code into the `main` branch. This will trigger the GitHub Actions CI/CD pipeline, which builds the Docker image, runs the test suite, and pushes the image to our Elastic Container Registry. The infrastructure will automatically perform a rolling update.

5. COMPLIANCE & LEGAL
Data Privacy Policy: TechSphere complies with GDPR and CCPA. Employee data and customer data must never be stored on local machines. All access to the production database requires MFA and VPN authentication.
Vendor Compliance Requirements: Any third-party vendor integrating with our APIs must sign the standard NDA and undergo a security audit by our internal InfoSec team.

6. PROJECT KNOWLEDGE
- Payment Module: The core payment gateway integration (Stripe/PayPal) was developed and is currently maintained by Sarah Jenkins (Lead Backend Engineer) and Alex Chen.
- Authentication Flow: We use OAuth 2.0 with JWT tokens. The documentation for the auth endpoints is located in the internal Confluence wiki under "Engineering / Auth Services".
